Chromebook

Chromebooks are computers students use for school work. Chromebooks are light, portable, and convenient. Chromebooks run on ChromeOS for their operating system. Chromebooks are centered around keeping everything on the cloud (allowing students to access their work from any Chromebook).

Is your Chromebook having problems? Re-enrolling your Chromebook might be able to fix them. After long periods of use Chromebooks may need to be re-enrolled. If your Chromebook is used by multiple people, it may need to be re-enrolled frequently.

How to log on a Chromebook

To log on to a Chromebook, you first turn it on. It will automatically open a Classlink login page. Enter your Classlink username and password and press sign in.

Does your Chromebook say "Your password has changed since the last time you signed in"? If you don't remeber your old password, try re-enrolling your Chromebook.

How To Re-enroll A Chromebook

Step 1:

Press Esc and ⟳ and power buttons all at the same time.

Step 2:

You should now see a message that says. “Please insert a recovery USB stick or card.” Press the ctrl + D keys at the same time.

Step 3:

Now you should see a message that says, “To turn OS verification Off, press Enter. Your system will reboot and local data will be cleared.” Press the enter key. It will load for a small ammount of time.

Step 4:

Now you should see a message that says, “Press ENTER to confirm you wish to turn os verification on. Your system will reboot and local data will be cleared." Press enter. It will load agian.

Step 5:

When the Welcome screen appears click "Get started."

Step 6:

Connect to the schools network. If you are using a network like WLCSD Guest you might have to click the "Sign in" button. If you are using a network like Student-L you will only have to type in the password.

Step 7:

After connecting to the network the re-enrollment process will finish itself. You might have to click a "Done" button when it finishes. If for some reason the Chromebook is unable to connect to the network, try closing and opening the chromebook screen to refresh it. If that does not work, try to log in agian or use a different network.

Chromebook Care

To clean a Chromebook, use a microfiber cloth and a disinfectant wipe for both the screen and keyboard. For hard to reach spots, use a can of air.

Chromebook Damage

If your Chromebook is damaged please take it down to the front office to get it looked at and tell someone in the IT department. If your school supports it, please submit a help ticket. The link to submit a ticket may be found in one of your teachers Google Classrooms.
